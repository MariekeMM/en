Minima Docta

Poster for the screening of the movie 'Traité de bave et d'éternité' as part of Minima Docta, a platform for social discussion and documentary cinema.

During the designing of this poster, I found inspiration in the hypergraphics made by Isidore Isou, founder of the lettrism movement. Lettrism is a french avant-garde movement founded by Isidore Isou, that originated from dada and surrealism. The hypergraphic is a central concept in lettrism and is a method by which the manner of expression of a character or a word via sound is extended with a visual facet.

Identity


This posterseries is a graphical interpretation of three movies made by director Stanley Kubrick: Killer's Kiss, Dr. Strangelove and The Killing. By using the violence that's present in the movies as a starting point, there has been a search for a mechanism that unites the different posters to a graphic identity. By searching for three colors that can be traced back to their respective black-and-white movies and combining the central murderweapon with a moviestill, the result is a visual way to create a graphic identity.

Typocopy


The central subject of this publication is reproduction.In the publication, many different famous designs coming from the history of graphic design are reproduced with a different chosen text. The choice has been made to use the story of 'The War of the Worlds' written by H.G. Wells, which is one of the most famous examples of invasion literature. Invasion literature originated from the period before the First World War and predicted the arrival of a bigger and stronger enemy that would invade and conquer the homeland. In 'The War of the Worlds' this enemy takes on the form of extraterrestrial beings. The writer however intended to use this format as a way of denouncing the europian colonies and criticizing the overall beheaviour of mankind in relation to each other. 


This is also the reason why the images used in this publication are not related at all with extraterrestrial beings. They are all images from the First and Second World War and the current batlle against terrorism in the Middle East. By laying the images on top of each other as different layers, the images are confronted with each other and connections are formed, the images created in different timeperiods begin to interact.

Ghent Art Book Fair


Posters created for the Ghent Art Book Fair 2017, organised by 019, Riot en Be-Part. Two posters had to be made, one announcement poster and one poster that could be used as decoration at the Art Book Fair. The two posters had to related to each other, so I chose signalisation as a central theme. The announcement poster with all the practical information can be used in each direction and will still be readable. It has the same color and form of a traffic sign announcing a detour. This poster can be used to indicate the direction in which the Art Book Fair can be found. At the location of the Art Book Fair the second poster can be found, that has the same format as a road sign announcing a town up ahead.
